ARCCSRL

Harry participated in the ARCCSRL continuous robotic limb project at MIT Media Lab. Human 2.0 At MIT

Harry's research at MIT Media Lab focused on designing and developing control interfaces for prosthetic limbs. He built a visually-assisted GUI on HoloLens for the ARCCSRL continuous robotic limb, enabling users to intuitively control the prosthetic through eye-gaze tracking and voice commands, significantly lowering the barrier to operation.

AR X ROBOTIC LIMB

Harry designed and implemented a visually-assisted user interface on HoloLens for the ARCCSRL continuous robotic limb developed at MIT Media Lab. By integrating eye-gaze tracking technology and voice commands, users can intuitively control the prosthetic without physical contact, pioneering a new mode of human-machine interaction.

Results And Takeaways

The goal of this research was to develop an innovative AR-assisted user interface for a continuous robotic limb. Through 22 user testing protocols, we collected and analyzed extensive interaction data. The final outcomes were exhibited at Harvard Innovation Labs and MIT Media Lab, generating broad discussion.

Key outcomes: AR eye-gaze control interface development, 22 user testing sessions completed, complex experimental data transformed into intuitive data visualizations, and research exhibited at Harvard & MIT.
